BODY:

TL;DR I find that both sycophantic and factual caving in Gemma 2 run through a doubt circuit, and characterise it as an answer revision/copying mechanism dependent on the plausibility of the alternative, that applies to both correct and incorrect feedback. I reproduce this across 2b, 9b, and 27b base models, and find that instruction-tuning at 2b and 9b amplifies the effect and redistributes the mechanism to distributed attention and MLPs, whilst the base mechanism remains, unused.

"Caving" is an expression of sycophancy 
Given plausible alternative answers to a question, models - including our selected Gemma 2 - "cave" and output the provided alternative. This can be correct (listening to a new fact), or incorrect (folding to incorrect user pressure). These have been called "progressive" and "regressive" sycophancy respectively. [cite]

Listening and folding require plausible alternatives
We split caving into listening, the model changing it's incorrect answer to a correct one, and folding, the model changing its correct answer to an incorrect one. 
[We keep an item?
